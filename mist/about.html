about

As you probably already have figured out, this is my lil' personal website to share my findings and creations about art, engineering, nature and other stuff. While doing that, I'd like to be as true to myself—how cheesy that might become—as possible. Why don't I just use a diary then? Well, the answer is simple: that's just less fun to me. And a lot of things I create are digital. And, now you can also take a look!

Now over to the technical aspects: I'm hosting this website myself, using a framework called Genie written in Julia. Julia may not be the most approachable language to write a webserver in, but I've been fiddling with it for a while now and it really grew on me.

the look

I decided to make this version of my website content focused, with a minimalistic style. I actually prefer this over bold colours and fonts, since the calmness reflects my personality more. I hope to still transfer warmth by my words and my art.

I took inspiration from the softer side of modern tech blog websites, together with dutch journalist/columnist websites. I find those websites pleasant to read and easy on the eyes. Next to that, it's also way easier to develop frontend that way: my frontend is 100% vanilla, because I don't like my HTML being a mess. This way a kid can click on inspect some day and hopefully discover a new CSS concept.
